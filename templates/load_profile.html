    Quick Help

    A load profile is a time series of electricity demand values, typically hourly. It shows how demand varies throughout the day, week, and year, accounting for patterns like peak hours, seasonality, and special days.

    Base Year Method: Uses patterns from a selected historical year and applies them to future years, scaling to match forecasted annual demand.

    ML Method: Uses machine learning to identify complex patterns in historical data, including weather correlations, to generate more nuanced future load profiles.

    Constraints ensure your generated load profiles maintain realistic patterns:

    • Monthly Maximum: Enforce expected monthly peak demand values
    • Monthly Average: Maintain expected monthly average demand
    • Seasonal Patterns: Preserve seasonal variations
    • Weekday/Weekend: Maintain distinction between weekday and weekend patterns

    Load factor is the ratio of average demand to peak demand, expressed as a percentage:

    • Higher load factors (closer to 100%) indicate more efficient utilization of system capacity
    • You can specify future improvements in load factor to model efficiency gains or behavioral changes
    • Typical load factors range from 60-80% for power systems
